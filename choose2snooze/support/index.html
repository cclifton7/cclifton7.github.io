Getting started

  1. Add an alarm. Tap + to create a new alarm and set the time.
  2. Choose a schedule. Pick Once, Daily, Week Days, Bi-Weekly, or Monthly — or tap specific dates on the calendar, and each date becomes its own alarm.
  3. Pick a sound. Use a built-in ringtone, a song from your music library, or record your own. See "song not showing?" below
  4. Set your snooze. Choose how long snooze lasts for that alarm.
  5. Allow alarms. The first time, allow Choose2Snooze to schedule alarms so they can alert you reliably.

Schedules

Once fires a single time, then is done. Daily repeats every day (or only the days you choose). Week Days covers Monday–Friday. Bi-Weekly and Monthly repeat on a longer cycle. To skip just the next ring without turning the alarm off, swipe right on it — it stays active for every ring after.

Custom sounds

Each alarm can use its own sound: a built-in ringtone, a song from your device's music library, or audio you record yourself (it loops while the alarm fires). You can set where a song starts so it opens on the right part of the track.

Wake up your home (HomeKit)

An alarm can trigger a HomeKit scene when it fires — for example, turning on the lights or opening the blinds. Add a scene from the alarm's Run Scene option. This is optional and only requests Home access if you use it.

Using your Apple Watch

Choose2Snooze includes a companion Apple Watch app so your alarms come with you.

Frequently asked questions

My alarm didn't go off. What happened?

Make sure you allowed Choose2Snooze to schedule alarms when prompted. You can check this in the iPhone Settings app under Choose2Snooze. Alarms use Apple's AlarmKit, so they alert even when your phone is on silent — but they do need that permission.

Why isn't my song showing up when I pick a sound?

Songs must be downloaded to your device before they appear. Open the Music app, find your track, and tap the download button (the cloud icon with an arrow). Streaming-only tracks that haven't been saved for offline use will not appear.

How do I skip just one occurrence?

Swipe right on the alarm and choose Skip Next. It skips only the next ring; the alarm stays active for every occurrence after that.

Can I set different snooze lengths for different alarms?

Yes. Snooze duration is set per alarm, so each one can snooze for as long or as short as you like.

How do I create alarms for specific future dates?

When adding an alarm, tap the specific dates you want on the calendar. You can select multiple dates, and each one becomes its own alarm.
